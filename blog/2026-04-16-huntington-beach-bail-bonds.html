Where Huntington Beach Arrests Are Processed

Someone arrested in Huntington Beach is transported to the Huntington Beach Police Department Jail at 2000 Main Street, Huntington Beach, CA 92648 (phone: 714-960-8825). The HBPD Jail is a full-service city lockup co-located with the Huntington Beach Police Department headquarters near City Hall, the Civic Center, and the Central Library — roughly a mile inland from the Huntington Beach Pier.

For serious felony charges or when the city facility is at capacity, defendants transfer to the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower Street, Santa Ana, CA 92703. This is the intake point for the Orange County Sheriff's Department (OCSD) jail system, which also includes the Theo Lacy Facility in Orange and the James A. Musick Facility in Irvine. Huntington Beach in Detail — What Makes This City's Bail Cases Different

Huntington Beach is a city-PD jurisdiction with its own full-service jail at 2000 Main Street, directly co-located with HBPD headquarters about a mile inland from the Huntington Beach Pier. HBPD handles the vast majority of city arrests and the jail has enough capacity to process most defendants locally before any transfer to the Orange County IRC in Santa Ana.

The city is built around tourism, the PCH, and an unusually dense downtown bar district centered on Main Street and 5th Street. Eleven million visitors a year pass through, and events like the U.S. Open of Surfing and Fourth of July celebrations generate multi-day arrest surges. HBPD runs heavy DUI enforcement on Pacific Coast Highway, Beach Boulevard, and Edinger Avenue. The bar corridor off Main Street contributes reliable public intoxication (PC 647(f)), battery (PC 242), and occasional assault (PC 245) arrests every weekend night.

A Huntington Beach specific nuance: the HBPD jail is local enough that misdemeanor bonds often release within 1–3 hours of posting — far faster than a county-level booking. For serious felonies or overflow, transfer to the OC IRC at 550 N Flower Street happens within 4–6 hours. The practical window is tight on weekend nights — posting at HBPD before that transfer saves significant hours on the release timeline. Typical Late-Night Arrest Scenarios in Huntington Beach

A PCH DUI stop on a Friday night — the classic HB arrest. Officers are staged between the pier and Beach Boulevard. First-offense bail $15,000 under VC 23152. Posting a bond at HBPD typically gets the defendant home before sunrise.

A bar-fight or battery arrest from the Main Street/5th Street corridor — PC 242 at $10,000, PC 245 if a weapon was involved at $30,000. HBPD processes these on-site; weekend volume can slow booking but bonds still move quickly.

A domestic violence call from the residential neighborhoods inland of Beach Boulevard — PC 273.5 books at $50,000 bail, HBPD handles the initial processing, with a mandatory arrest policy and a likely criminal protective order (PC 136.2) at arraignment.

How a Huntington Beach Bail Bond Actually Works

You call (626) 478-1062. We confirm the bail amount from the Orange County bail schedule — huntington beach bail bonds typically range from $5,000 for petty theft (PC 484) to $50,000 for domestic violence (PC 273.5) and $100,000 for DUI with injury (VC 23153). You pay Angels Bail Bonds a 10% premium — the state-set rate under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. We post the full bail amount with the jail as a surety bond. The defendant is released, typically 1–4 hours at HBPD Jail and 4–8 hours at the Orange County Intake and Release Center (IRC).

The 10% is a fee for services — it is a premium paid to the bondsman, not a loan. When the case concludes and all court appearances are made, the bond is exonerated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does a Huntington Beach arrest actually get processed?

Huntington Beach arrests are processed at the HBPD Jail at 2000 Main Street. Misdemeanor bonds post here with 1–3 hour release typical. Serious felonies transfer to the Orange County IRC at 550 N Flower Street in Santa Ana within 4–6 hours.

How much does a bail bond actually cost in Huntington Beach?

The bail bond fee is 10% of the total bail amount under California Insurance Code § 1800.4. For a first-offense DUI with bail set at $15,000, you pay $1,500. That 10% is our fee for posting the full bond — it is not refunded at case conclusion. Payment plans are available for qualifying clients.

Can Huntington Beach bail be posted the same night?

Yes, in most cases. California does not impose a mandatory overnight hold. Once the booking process is complete and the Orange County bail schedule amount is assigned, a licensed bondsman can post the bond immediately. Most Huntington Beach DUI and misdemeanor arrests result in same-night or early-morning release.
